FY 2024-25 / AY 2025-26

SECTION 80C — DEDUCTIONS FOR SPECIFIED INVESTMENTS AND EXPENSES
==============================================================

Section 80C of the Income Tax Act, 1961 allows deductions up to ₹1,50,000 per financial year 
for investments and payments made towards specified instruments.

Eligible investments and payments under Section 80C:

1. Life Insurance Premium (LIC)
   - Premiums paid for life insurance policies on self, spouse, or children
   - Premium must not exceed 10% of sum assured (20% for pre-2012 policies)
   - Applicable for: HDFC Life, LIC, ICICI Prudential, SBI Life, Max Life, Bajaj Allianz, etc.
   - Keywords: LIC PREMIUM, LIFE INS, INSURANCE PREMIUM, LIC OF INDIA

2. Public Provident Fund (PPF)
   - Contributions to PPF account (minimum ₹500, maximum ₹1,50,000 per year)
   - Lock-in period: 15 years
   - Keywords: PPF, PUBLIC PROVIDENT FUND

3. Employee Provident Fund (EPF)
   - Employee's contribution to EPF (employer's contribution is exempt but not deductible)
   - Keywords: EPF, PROVIDENT FUND, PF CONTRIBUTION

4. Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS) Mutual Funds
   - Investments in ELSS mutual funds (3-year lock-in)
   - Keywords: ELSS, SIP, AXIS LONG TERM, MIRAE ASSET TAX SAVER, ADITYA BIRLA TAX RELIEF, 
     QUANT TAX PLAN, DSP TAX SAVER, HDFC TAXSAVER, ICICI PRUDENTIAL LONG TERM EQUITY

5. National Savings Certificate (NSC)
   - Investments in NSC at post office
   - Keywords: NSC, NATIONAL SAVINGS CERTIFICATE

6. 5-Year Fixed Deposit (Tax Saving FD)
   - Fixed deposits with scheduled banks for minimum 5 years
   - Keywords: TAX SAVER FD, 5 YEAR FD, FIXED DEPOSIT

7. Home Loan Principal Repayment
   - Principal component of EMI on home loan for purchase/construction
   - Keywords: HOME LOAN EMI, HOUSING LOAN, HDFC HOME LOAN, SBI HOME LOAN, 
     LIC HOUSING, BAJAJ HOUSING

8. Children's Tuition Fees
   - Full-time education fees for up to 2 children
   - Fees paid to schools, colleges, universities in India only
   - Does NOT include donation, development fees, or coaching fees
   - Keywords: SCHOOL FEES, TUITION FEES, COLLEGE FEES, UNIVERSITY FEES, 
     CBSE SCHOOL, ICSE SCHOOL

9. Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY)
   - Contributions for girl child account
   - Keywords: SUKANYA SAMRIDDHI, SSY

10. National Pension System (NPS) — Tier 1
    - Employee contributions to NPS Tier-1
    - Additional deduction of ₹50,000 available under Section 80CCD(1B) over 80C limit
    - Keywords: NPS, NATIONAL PENSION SYSTEM, PFRDA

AGGREGATE LIMIT for 80C + 80CCC + 80CCD(1): MAXIMUM ₹1,50,000


SECTION 80CCC — PENSION FUND CONTRIBUTIONS
==========================================
Contributions to pension funds of LIC or any other insurer.
Combined limit with 80C and 80CCD(1): ₹1,50,000


SECTION 80CCD(1B) — ADDITIONAL NPS DEDUCTION
=============================================
Additional deduction of up to ₹50,000 for contributions to NPS Tier-1.
This is OVER AND ABOVE the ₹1,50,000 limit of Section 80C.
Total effective limit: ₹2,00,000 (₹1,50,000 + ₹50,000)


SECTION 80D — HEALTH INSURANCE PREMIUMS
========================================

Section 80D allows deduction for health insurance premiums paid.

Limits:
- Self, spouse, dependent children: Up to ₹25,000 per year
- If any member is a senior citizen (60+ years): Up to ₹50,000 per year
- Additional for parents (non-senior): Up to ₹25,000 per year
- Additional for senior citizen parents: Up to ₹50,000 per year
- Maximum possible deduction: ₹1,00,000 (self senior + parents senior)
- Preventive health check-up: Up to ₹5,000 (included in above limits)

Eligible payments:
- Health insurance premiums (Mediclaim)
- Critical illness riders/plans
- Payments to CGHS or approved medical schemes

Insurance providers: Star Health, HDFC Ergo, Niva Bupa (Max Bupa), Care Health, 
ICICI Lombard, New India Assurance, Oriental Insurance, United India Insurance, 
Bajaj Allianz Health, Reliance Health

Keywords: MEDICLAIM, HEALTH INSURANCE, STAR HEALTH, HDFC ERGO, MAX BUPA, NIVA BUPA,
CARE HEALTH, ICICI LOMBARD HEALTH, NEW INDIA HEALTH, HEALTH PREMIUM, CRITICAL ILLNESS


SECTION 80E — EDUCATION LOAN INTEREST
======================================

Section 80E provides deduction for interest paid on education loan.

Key features:
- Only INTEREST component is deductible (not principal)
- No upper limit on deduction amount
- Available for 8 consecutive years from the year repayment starts
- Loan must be taken from a financial institution or approved charitable institution
- For higher education of self, spouse, children, or student for whom taxpayer is legal guardian
- Covers any field of study after passing Senior Secondary Exam

Eligible institutions for loans: Banks (SBI, HDFC Bank, Axis Bank, ICICI Bank, 
PNB, BOB, Canara Bank), NBFCs, Vidya Lakshmi portal loans

Keywords: EDUCATION LOAN, STUDENT LOAN, STUDY LOAN, EDUCATION LOAN EMI, 
EDU LOAN INTEREST, HDFC EDUCATION LOAN, SBI SCHOLAR LOAN, VIDYA LAKSHMI


SECTION 80G — DONATIONS TO CHARITABLE INSTITUTIONS
====================================================

Section 80G allows deduction for donations to approved funds and institutions.

Categories of deductions:

1. 100% deduction WITHOUT qualifying limit:
   - Prime Minister's National Relief Fund (PMNRF)
   - Prime Minister's Citizen Assistance and Relief in Emergency Situations Fund (PM-CARES)
   - National Defence Fund
   - National Children's Fund
   - Jawaharlal Nehru Memorial Fund
   - Rajiv Gandhi Foundation

2. 50% deduction WITHOUT qualifying limit:
   - Prime Minister's Drought Relief Fund
   - National Cultural Fund
   - Technology Development and Application Fund
   - National Illness Assistance Fund

3. 100% deduction WITH qualifying limit (10% of adjusted gross total income):
   - Donations to approved associations for rural development
   - Approved colleges and universities

4. 50% deduction WITH qualifying limit (10% of adjusted gross total income):
   - Donations to any other approved institution
   - Local authority, government, charitable trusts, NGOs with 80G certification

Important: Cash donations above ₹2,000 are NOT eligible for deduction.
Donations must be made by cheque, demand draft, or digital payment.

Keywords: DONATION, PMNRF, PM CARES, NGO, CHARITABLE TRUST, CRY, UNICEF INDIA,
GIVE INDIA, SMILE FOUNDATION, HELPAGE INDIA, AKSHAYA PATRA, PRATHAM, TEACH FOR INDIA


SECTION 80GG — RENT PAID (FOR THOSE WITHOUT HRA)
==================================================

Section 80GG is for individuals who do NOT receive House Rent Allowance (HRA) 
but pay rent for their accommodation.

Conditions:
- Taxpayer must not receive HRA from employer
- Taxpayer, spouse, or minor child should not own a house in the city of employment
- Must file Form 10BA declaration

Deduction amount: Minimum of:
a) ₹5,000 per month (₹60,000 per year)
b) 25% of total adjusted income
c) Actual rent paid minus 10% of total adjusted income

Keywords: RENT PAYMENT, HOUSE RENT, FLAT RENT, APARTMENT RENT, 
RENT PAID, RENT TRANSFER, LANDLORD RENT


SECTION 24(B) — HOME LOAN INTEREST (INCOME FROM HOUSE PROPERTY)
================================================================

Section 24(b) allows deduction for interest paid on home loan.

Limits and conditions:
- Self-occupied property: Up to ₹2,00,000 per year
- Let-out property: No upper limit (actual interest paid)
- Pre-construction interest: Deductible in 5 equal installments from year of completion
- Loan must be taken after April 1, 1999 for purchase/construction

This deduction is under "Income from House Property" — not Chapter VI-A.
Combined benefit with Section 80C (principal) and 24B (interest) covers full EMI.

Lenders: HDFC Ltd, LIC Housing Finance, Bajaj Housing Finance, PNB Housing, 
SBI Home Loan, ICICI Home Finance, Axis Bank, Bank of Baroda

Keywords: HOME LOAN INTEREST, HOUSING LOAN EMI, HDFC HOME LOAN, LIC HOUSING,
BAJAJ HOUSING, PNB HOUSING, HOME LOAN EMI, GRUH FINANCE


SECTION 37(1) — BUSINESS/PROFESSIONAL EXPENSES
===============================================

Section 37(1) allows deduction for expenses incurred wholly and exclusively for 
business or profession purposes.

Eligible expenses:
- Professional subscriptions and memberships
- Business travel and conveyance
- Professional development courses/certifications
- Office supplies and equipment (for home office use in profession)
- Internet and phone bills (professional use portion)
- Professional books and journals

Not eligible: Personal expenses, capital expenditure, penalties, fines

Keywords: PROFESSIONAL SUBSCRIPTION, BUSINESS EXPENSE, PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT,
ONLINE COURSE, CERTIFICATION FEE, AWS CERTIFICATION, UDEMY, COURSERA (for professionals)


SECTION 80TTA — INTEREST ON SAVINGS ACCOUNT
============================================

Section 80TTA allows deduction for interest earned on savings bank accounts.
- Limit: ₹10,000 per year
- For senior citizens: Section 80TTB applies with limit of ₹50,000


SECTION 80U — DISABILITY DEDUCTION
====================================

For individuals with disabilities:
- 40% to 79% disability: ₹75,000 fixed deduction
- 80% or more disability (severe): ₹1,25,000 fixed deduction


SECTION 80DD — DISABLED DEPENDENT
====================================

For expenses on medical treatment and maintenance of disabled dependent:
- 40% to 79% disability: ₹75,000
- 80%+ disability (severe): ₹1,25,000


NEW TAX REGIME vs OLD TAX REGIME
=================================

From FY 2023-24, the New Tax Regime is the DEFAULT regime.
Under NEW TAX REGIME: Most deductions (80C, 80D, 80E, 80G, HRA, LTA) are NOT available.
Under OLD TAX REGIME: All the above deductions are available.

Taxpayers must explicitly opt for the OLD regime to claim these deductions.
Standard deduction of ₹50,000 is available under BOTH regimes for salaried employees.

FINANCIAL YEAR AND ASSESSMENT YEAR
====================================

Financial Year (FY) 2024-25 = April 1, 2024 to March 31, 2025
Assessment Year (AY) 2025-26 = April 1, 2025 to March 31, 2026

The deductions are claimed while filing ITR for AY 2025-26 (i.e., for income earned in FY 2024-25).


TAX SLABS UNDER OLD REGIME (FY 2024-25)
=========================================

Income up to ₹2,50,000: Nil
₹2,50,001 to ₹5,00,000: 5%
₹5,00,001 to ₹10,00,000: 20%
Above ₹10,00,000: 30%

Plus: 4% Health & Education Cess on total tax
Plus: Surcharge for income above ₹50 lakh

Standard Deduction: ₹50,000 for salaried employees

MAXIMUM TAX SAVING THROUGH DEDUCTIONS (OLD REGIME):
- Section 80C: ₹1,50,000 × 30% = ₹45,000
- Section 80D: ₹25,000 × 30% = ₹7,500
- Section 80CCD(1B): ₹50,000 × 30% = ₹15,000
- Section 24B: ₹2,00,000 × 30% = ₹60,000
- Total potential tax saving: ₹1,27,500+ (at 30% slab)
